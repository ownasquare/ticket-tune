# TicketTune Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

TicketTune welcomes contributors of every background and experience level. We are committed to a
respectful, inclusive, and harassment-free project where technical disagreement stays focused on
the work and evidence.

## Our standards

Positive participation includes:

- being considerate, specific, and constructive;
- welcoming questions and explaining unfamiliar concepts without condescension;
- respecting privacy, model and dataset licenses, and honest proof boundaries;
- accepting correction and taking responsibility for mistakes; and
- prioritizing the safety and accessibility of the wider community.

Unacceptable behavior includes harassment, discrimination, threats, sexualized attention,
personal attacks, deliberate intimidation, publishing private information, sustained disruption,
or pressuring anyone to disclose credentials, private datasets, or identifying information.

## Scope

This policy applies in the repository, issues, pull requests, reviews, discussions, project events,
and other spaces where someone is representing TicketTune. It also applies to private project
communications when the behavior materially affects community safety.

## Reporting

Do not put sensitive conduct details or personal information in a public issue. Use GitHub's private
vulnerability-reporting channel when available and identify the report as a conduct matter. If no
private channel is available, open a minimal issue asking a maintainer to establish one without
including names, screenshots, or incident details.

Reports should identify the relevant project space, describe the behavior and impact, and include
only evidence the reporter is authorized to share. Maintainers will limit disclosure to the people
needed to assess and resolve the report.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit or remove content, issue a private warning, limit participation, temporarily
suspend access, or permanently ban a participant. The response will be proportional to the harm,
pattern, and risk of recurrence. Retaliation against someone who reports or helps investigate a
concern is itself a violation.

Maintainers with a conflict of interest must step away from the decision. Good-faith reports will
not be punished when the available evidence does not support enforcement.
